The Southwest and Beyond

Hiking has become such a huge part of my life, especially living in this part of the country. I’ve spent a lot of time exploring the rugged beauty of Texas State Parks, but I always love crossing borders to see what our neighbors have to offer. Whether it’s the high-altitude trails in Colorado, the red rocks of Arizona, or the hauntingly beautiful landscapes of New Mexico, every state has its own unique energy. There’s something special about getting away from the city and realizing how much diverse wilderness we actually have right in our backyard.

The Ultimate Trail Bucket List

While I’ve seen some incredible places, my hiking bucket list is still pretty long. I’m dying to get up to Wyoming and the Dakotas to experience that wide-open, northern frontier feel. I also want to head in the opposite direction and hit the East Coast mountains—the Appalachians have a completely different, lush vibe compared to the dry heat of the Southwest. For me, the goal is to see as many different types of terrain as possible; every new trail feels like a fresh chapter in a much bigger story.

Good for the Body and the Soul

The best thing about hiking is that it’s just as good for your head as it is for your legs. It’s some of the best exercise you can get, but it doesn't feel like a chore when the views are that good. It’s also a surprisingly cheap hobby—once you invest in a solid pair of boots, the trails are basically free. Whenever life gets a little too loud or stressful, hitting a trail is the perfect way to clear my mind and reconnect with what matters. It’s great for the soul, and there’s no better feeling than reaching a summit and looking out at the world.
